Section 6: Vibration Diagnostics and Bearing Maintenance

6.1 ACCEPTABLE VIBRATION LEVELS
Normal operation: < 4.5 mm/s RMS at bearing housing
Alert level: 4.5–7.1 mm/s RMS — schedule inspection within 72 hours
Trip level: > 7.1 mm/s RMS — stop pump and investigate before restart

6.2 COMMON CAUSES OF ELEVATED VIBRATION
- Shaft misalignment between motor and pump coupling
- Bearing wear or lubrication failure
- Cavitation due to insufficient NPSH
- Loose foundation bolts or pipe strain on suction/discharge

6.3 DIAGNOSTIC SEQUENCE (VENDOR RECOMMENDED)
Step 1: Verify suction/discharge valve positions and flow rate
Step 2: Check coupling alignment (laser alignment preferred)
Step 3: Measure bearing temperature — if rising with vibration, suspect alignment
Step 4: Inspect mechanical seal for dry-running damage
Step 5: Only replace bearings after alignment is confirmed within 0.05 mm

6.4 BEARING REPLACEMENT NOTES
Use SKF 6312-C3 bearings. Do not install new bearings on a misaligned shaft —
expected bearing life drops by 60% per ISO 281 calculation.
