NEAR-MISS INCIDENT REPORT
Report No: NM-2025-018          Classification: Near Miss (no injury, no release)
Date/Time of Event: 2025-05-22, 14:05
Location: Crude Distillation Unit, Heat Exchanger HX-201
Reported by: D. Souza (Fitter)        Investigated by: S. Iyer

1. SUMMARY
During preparation to open heat exchanger HX-201 for cleaning, the channel-side
bleed valve was opened and released a small puff of trapped hydrocarbon vapour.
Work was stopped immediately. No injury, no sustained release, no fire.

2. SEQUENCE OF EVENTS
- HX-201 was isolated and believed depressurised per the work pack.
- The de-pressurisation step had not been independently verified before the bleed
  valve was cracked open.
- Residual pressure (~0.4 barg) pushed vapour out of the bleed point.
- Gas test had been taken at the manway but NOT at the bleed point.

3. IMMEDIATE CAUSE
Incomplete positive isolation verification before breaking containment on HX-201.

4. ROOT/CONTRIBUTING FACTORS
- Permit to Work checklist did not require a gas test at every break point
  (gap against OISD-STD-105 intent on confined/contained energy).
- Depressurisation confirmation relied on a single reading.

5. RECOMMENDATIONS (tracked to closure per OISD-GDN-206)
R1. Update the Permit to Work checklist to require gas testing at each point of
    breaking containment, including bleed and drain valves. Owner: P. Rao.
R2. Add a two-person verification of depressurisation for shell-and-tube
    exchangers. Owner: R. Mehta.
R3. Toolbox talk on stored-energy hazards for HX-201 and similar exchangers.

6. STATUS
Open. Target closure 2025-06-30. Equipment HX-201 returned to isolation pending
revised permit.
